Overview of the Uto-Aztecan languages

The Uto-Aztecan language family is a primary language family whose languages are spoken by over a million people in the United States, Mexico, and El Salvador, and Nicaragua. It is typically divided into northern and southern branches; the languages of the northern branch are spoken mostly in the southwestern US, and those of the much larger southern branch are mostly spoken in Mexico.

Northern languages

The best-known northern languages are Hopi, spoken in Arizona, and Comanche, with a few remaining speakers in Oklahoma.

Southern languages

The major southern language is Nahuatl, the language of the former Aztec empire, and it was formerly spoken throughout Mesoamerica as a lingua franca. Modern Nahuatl is divided into many dialects, not all of which are mutually intelligible. Other major southern languages include Huichol, Mayo, and Tepehuan.
